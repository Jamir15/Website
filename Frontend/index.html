About the System

🌡️ System Overview

The Classroom Evaluation Parametric Instrument and Digital Twin System is an integrated environmental monitoring platform designed to track, analyze, and visualize classroom thermal conditions in real time. The system combines multiple sensor positions, a 3D digital twin visualization, and a decision support dashboard for classroom environmental monitoring.

📍 Room 1 Monitoring Layout

Room 1 uses four sensor positions for environmental monitoring: front, back, left, and right. Temperature and humidity from the available nodes are combined to produce the averaged Room 1 environmental condition shown on the dashboard.

Thermal visualization remains connected to the front node, where the MLX90640 thermal imaging module is installed.

⏰ Peak Heat Hours

The system monitors and alerts users during Peak Heat Hours, typically occurring between 11:00 AM and 4:00 PM. During these hours, the risk of heat-related discomfort becomes more significant.

Alert Trigger: An automatic notification banner appears when the current time falls within peak heat hours.

🧮 Heat Index Calculation

The system uses the Rothfusz Regression Formula for Heat Index computation, with fallback to the Steadman approximation when the conditions are outside the intended Rothfusz operating range.

Rothfusz Regression Formula:

HI = -42.379 + 2.04901523×T + 10.14333127×R - 0.22475541×T×R - 0.00683783×T² - 0.05481717×R² + 0.00122874×T²×R + 0.00085282×T×R² - 0.00000199×T²×R²

Where: T = Temperature (°F), R = Relative Humidity (%), HI = Heat Index (°F)

Calculation Conditions:

  • Below 20°C: Heat Index equals air temperature
  • 20°C to 26.7°C: Steadman simple approximation
  • Above 26.7°C: Full Rothfusz regression with humidity adjustments

⚠️ Heat Index Categories & Advisories

Normal < 27°C

Comfortable conditions. No heat-related risk.

Caution 27°C – 32°C

Possible fatigue with prolonged exposure. Low risk, but still uncomfortable.

Extreme Caution 33°C – 41°C

Higher chance of heat cramps and possible heat exhaustion. Extra hydration and breaks needed.

Danger 42°C – 51°C

Likely heat cramps and heat exhaustion. Heat stroke becomes possible with prolonged exposure.

Extreme Danger 52°C and above

Heat stroke highly likely. Immediate protective measures required.

Cabuyao CDRRMO Heat Index Threshold Data

Reference thresholds from the Cabuyao City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO) report titled "Threshold Data on Heat Indices", including PAGASA heat index thresholds and related environmental factors used for advisories and operational decisions.

Heat Index (°C) Category Advisory Notes
27°C – 32°C Caution Possible fatigue with prolonged exposure. Low risk, but still uncomfortable.
33°C – 41°C Extreme Caution Higher chance of heat cramps. Possible heat exhaustion. Extra hydration and breaks needed. Vulnerable groups are more at risk.
42°C – 51°C Danger Likely heat cramps and heat exhaustion. Heat stroke becomes possible with prolonged exposure. Outdoor activities become risky. This is often the reference point the City of Cabuyao uses when considering suspending classes.
52°C and above Extreme Danger Heat stroke highly likely. Very unsafe for prolonged exposure. Immediate protective measures required.

1. Air Temperature Thresholds

High temperature (e.g., 33°C and above) increases heat stress on the body.

Used to identify when heat index is likely to reach dangerous levels.

2. Relative Humidity Thresholds

High humidity (with 60% and above) reduces the body's ability to cool through sweating.

Humidity combined with temperature is what forms the heat index.

4. Wind Speed Thresholds

Low wind speed increases heat stress because it reduces natural cooling.

Calm conditions (below 5 km/h) can aggravate high heat index levels.

5. Solar Radiation / Sun Exposure

High sunlight exposure further increases perceived heat.

Outdoor activities become more dangerous even at lower heat index values.

6. Time-of-Day Threshold

Heat index is highest between 11:00 AM to 4:00 PM.

Important for class scheduling, PE activities, and outdoor school events.

7. Indoor Temperature and Ventilation Thresholds

Classrooms above 35°C and with poor ventilation increase health risks for students and staff.

Used by school heads to decide on suspending in-person classes.

🧩 Scalability

The interface preserves a Room 2 navigation entry to reflect the system’s scalable architecture. While Room 2 is currently reserved for future expansion, the backend, Firestore structure, dashboard routing, and overall system design are prepared for multi-room deployment.

🔧 Hardware Components

  • ESP32-based Nodes – Distributed environmental sensing and wireless transmission
  • AHT20 Sensor – Temperature and humidity measurement
  • MLX90640 – 32×24 thermal imaging array for front thermal visualization

💻 Software Architecture

Backend: Node.js, Express.js, Firebase Admin SDK
Frontend: Three.js, Chart.js, Vanilla JavaScript
Database: Firebase Firestore
AI Service: OpenAI GPT-4o-mini for natural language explanation
3D Assets: GLB/GLTF classroom digital twin model
