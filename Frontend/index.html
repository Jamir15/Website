About the System

🌡️ System Overview

The Classroom Evaluation Parametric Instrument and Digital Twin System is an integrated environmental monitoring platform designed to track, analyze, and visualize thermal conditions in real-time. The system combines IoT sensor networks, 3D digital twin visualization, and AI-powered decision support to ensure optimal indoor environmental quality.

⏰ Peak Heat Hours

The system monitors and alerts users during Peak Heat Hours, typically occurring between 11:00 AM and 4:00 PM (11:00 - 16:00). During these hours, solar radiation is at its maximum, increasing the risk of heat-related stress even in indoor environments.

Alert Trigger: Automatic notification banner displays when current time falls within peak heat hours.

🧮 Heat Index Calculation

The system uses the National Weather Service (NWS) Rothfusz Regression Formula for calculating the Heat Index, with fallback to the Steadman approximation for lower temperatures.

Rothfusz Regression Formula:

HI = -42.379 + 2.04901523×T + 10.14333127×R - 0.22475541×T×R - 0.00683783×T² - 0.05481717×R² + 0.00122874×T²×R + 0.00085282×T×R² - 0.00000199×T²×R²

Where: T = Temperature (°F), R = Relative Humidity (%), HI = Heat Index (°F)

Calculation Conditions:

  • Below 20°C: Heat Index equals air temperature
  • 20°C - 26.7°C: Steadman simple approximation
  • Above 26.7°C: Full Rothfusz regression with humidity adjustments

⚠️ Heat Index Categories & Advisories

Normal < 27°C

Comfortable conditions. No heat-related risk.

Caution 27°C - 32°C

Fatigue possible with prolonged exposure.

Extreme Caution 32°C - 41°C

Heat cramps and heat exhaustion possible.

Danger 41°C - 54°C

Heat cramps likely. Heat exhaustion probable.

Extreme Danger > 54°C

Heat stroke highly likely with continued exposure.

Cabuyao CDRRMO Heat Index Threshold Data

Reference thresholds from the Cabuyao City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO) report titled "Threshold Data on Heat Indices", including PAGASA heat index thresholds and related environmental factors used for advisories and operational decisions.

Heat Index (°C) Category Advisory Notes
27°C – 32°C Caution Possible fatigue with prolonged exposure. Low risk, but still uncomfortable.
33°C – 41°C Extreme Caution Higher chance of heat cramps; possible heat exhaustion. Extra hydration and breaks needed. Vulnerable groups (children, elderly) are more at risk.
42°C – 51°C Danger Likely heat cramps and heat exhaustion. Heat stroke becomes possible with prolonged exposure. Outdoor activities become risky. Often used as a reference point when considering suspending classes.
≥ 52°C Extreme Danger Heat stroke highly likely. Very unsafe for outdoor activities and prolonged exposure. Immediate protective measures required.

1. Air Temperature Thresholds

High temperature (e.g., 33°C and above) increases heat stress on the body.

Used to identify when heat index is likely to reach dangerous levels.

2. Relative Humidity Thresholds

High humidity (with 60% and above) reduces the body's ability to cool through sweating.

Humidity combined with temperature is what forms the heat index.

4. Wind Speed Thresholds

Low wind speed increases heat stress because it reduces natural cooling.

Calm conditions (below 5 km/h) can aggravate high heat index levels.

5. Solar Radiation / Sun Exposure

High sunlight exposure further increases perceived heat.

Outdoor activities become more dangerous even at lower heat index values.

6. Time-of-Day Threshold

Heat index is highest between 11:00 AM to 4:00 PM.

Important for class scheduling, PE activities, and outdoor school events.

7. Indoor Temperature and Ventilation Thresholds

Classrooms above 35°C and with poor ventilation increase health risks for students and staff.

Used by school heads to decide on suspending in-person classes.

🔧 Hardware Components

  • ESP32 Microcontroller - Main processing unit with WiFi/Bluetooth connectivity
  • AHT20 Sensor - Digital temperature and humidity measurement
  • MLX90640 - 32×24 pixel thermal imaging array for heat mapping
  • TFT Display - Local real-time data visualization

💻 Software Architecture

Backend: Node.js, Express.js, Firebase Admin SDK
Frontend: Three.js (3D), Chart.js (Graphs), Vanilla JavaScript
Database: Firebase Firestore (Real-time NoSQL)
AI Service: OpenAI GPT-4o-mini for natural language explanations
3D Assets: GLB/GLTF format classroom models

📡 Data Flow Architecture

  1. Sensors (ESP32 + AHT20 + MLX90640) collect environmental data
  2. Data transmitted via WiFi to Firebase Firestore
  3. Backend server (Node.js/Express) processes and computes Heat Index
  4. 3D Digital Twin receives thermal data for volumetric heat visualization
  5. Dashboard displays real-time metrics, charts, and AI-powered advisories
  6. AI Assistant provides contextual explanations based on current conditions

🔥 Thermal Heatmap Visualization

The system employs volumetric heat propagation using 12 depth slices that simulate heat diffusion from the front wall into the classroom space. Each slice has:

  • Exponential decay based on distance from source
  • Time-lagged updates creating wave/ripple effects
  • HSL color mapping (Blue → Red) for temperature gradients
  • Emissive glow effects for realistic heat radiation

🛡️ Fallback & Safety Mechanisms

  • Backend Unavailable: Local heat index estimation using simplified formula
  • Sensor Timeout: 6-second watchdog timer with visual degradation
  • WorldTimeAPI Failure: Automatic fallback to browser local time
  • AI Service Down: Structured text response with current sensor values

🏢 Multi-Room Architecture

The system supports monitoring multiple rooms simultaneously with independent:

  • Sensor data streams (front and back sensors per room)
  • Thermal imaging feeds (32×24 pixel arrays per room)
  • Heat Index calculations and advisories per zone
  • Real-time switching between rooms via navigation panel
